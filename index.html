About

I am a global health economist who studies the causes of population health improvement. My research focuses on several areas of population health and noncommunicable diseases (NCDs): the population-level causes and consequences of NCDs, effective public health policies for NCD prevention and treatment, and health system readiness for NCDs. I have methodological expertise in observational data analysis, applied microeconomics, and demographic methods. My goal is to generate rigorous evidence on effective strategies for improving health in resource-constrained settings.

I am currently a National Institute on Aging T32 postdoctoral scholar in the Demography Department at the University of California, Berkeley. I completed my Ph.D. in Health Policy (Economics) and M.S. in Statistics at Stanford University.
