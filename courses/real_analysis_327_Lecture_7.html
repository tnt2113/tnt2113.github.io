Lecture 7

Convergence of sequences

Definition: a sequence is a function, $f: \mathbb{N} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}, n \rightarrow f(n)$

Definition: The sequence $\big\{{a_{n}}\big\}_{n}$ converges if $\exists a \in \mathbb{R}$ such that $\forall \epsilon > 0, \exists N \in \mathbb{N}, n \geq N \implies |a_{n} - a| < \epsilon$. We also write: $\lim_{x\to\infty} a_{n} = a$

The Comparison Lemma: let two sequences, $a_{n}, b_{n}, a,b \in \mathbb{R}$. Suppose $\lim_{n\to\infty} a_{n} = a$ and $\exists c > 0, N_{0} \in \mathbb{N}, \forall n \geq N_{0}, |b_{n}-b| \leq c |a_{n} - a|$. Then $\lim_{n\to\infty} b_{n} = b$

Algebraic Limit Rules

Suppose $\lim_{n\to\infty} a_{n} = a$ and $\lim_{n\to\infty} b_{n} = b$ for two sequences $\big\{{a_{n}}\big\}_{n}$ and $\big\{{b_{n}}\big\}_{n}$. Then:

  1. $\lim_{n\to\infty} (a_{n} + b_{n}) = a + b = \lim_{n\to\infty} a_{n} + \lim_{n\to\infty} b_{n})$
  2. $\lim_{n\to\infty} (a_{n} \cdot b_{n}) = a \cdot b = \lim_{n\to\infty} a_{n} \cdot \lim_{n\to\infty} b_{n})$

If in addition $b_{n} \neq 0, \forall n \in \mathbb{N}$ and $b \neq 0$ then:

  1. $\big\{{\frac{1}{b_{n}}\big\}_{n}$ converges and $\lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{1}{b_{n}} =\frac{1}{b} $
  2. $\big\{{\frac{a_{n}}{b_{n}}\big\}_{n}$ converges and $\lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{{a_{n}}{b_{n}} =\frac{a}{b} $
